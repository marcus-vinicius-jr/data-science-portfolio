This project applies exploratory data analysis and business storytelling to the Kaggle **Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist**, framed from the perspective of a Head of Operations who needs to turn charts into decisions.

The notebook combines:
- business framing and hypotheses stated **before** looking at the data,
- data understanding and quality checks across multiple relational tables,
- exploratory data analysis (sales/revenue, delivery, reviews, geography),
- formal hypothesis validation with statistical tests,
- and business storytelling: findings, implications and recommended actions.

---

## Business Question

Where are orders concentrated geographically, how does delivery performance affect customer satisfaction, and which product categories and payment patterns drive revenue, with what statistical confidence?

---

## Dataset

- Source: [Kaggle - Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/olistbr/brazilian-ecommerce)
- Coverage: ~100k orders placed between 2016 and 2018
- Rows after master join: 96,478 delivered orders
- Key variables: `order_purchase_timestamp`, `delivery_delay_days`, `review_score`, `total_payment`, `max_installments`, `customer_state`, `product_category_name_english`

Data is downloaded in the notebook via Kaggle API into `data/`.

## Hypotheses & Test Results

| # | Hypothesis | Test | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| H1 | Late deliveries get significantly lower review scores | One-tailed t-test | **Confirmed** - t = -131.85, p ≈ 0, Cohen's d = 1.47 |
| H2 | Southeast region accounts for the majority of revenue | Descriptive (proportion) | **Confirmed** - 64.6% revenue share |
| H3 | Higher installments associate with higher order value | Pearson correlation | **Confirmed (moderate)** - r = 0.374, p ≈ 0 |
| H4 | Review scores differ across product categories | One-way ANOVA | **Confirmed** - F = 32.54, p = 1.13e-57 |

---

## Key Findings

- **Delivery reliability is the dominant driver of satisfaction**: late orders average a 2.27 review score vs. 4.29 for on-time/early orders, a statistically and practically huge gap (Cohen's d = 1.47).
- **Revenue and satisfaction are not aligned geographically**: the Southeast holds 64.6% of revenue, but RJ - its #2 state by revenue - sits below the 4.0 average-review benchmark.
- **Installments correlate with order value, but only moderately** (r = 0.374); other factors (category, freight, customer profile) also matter.
- **Product category drives satisfaction independently of delivery**: telephony and watches_gifts show the widest spread toward low scores; auto, computers_accessories and furniture_decor cluster higher and tighter.
- **Underperformance compounds across metrics**: a composite operational ranking (revenue + avg review + on-time rate) shows that the bottom states (AL, MA, SE, CE, PA) rank low on all three dimensions simultaneously — AL's late-delivery rate (21.4%) is 4x SP's (4.5%).
- **MCA confirms delivery as the dominant categorical axis**: in a perceptual map of payment type, region, review level and delivery status, `Late` is the most extreme coordinate by far — separated from all other categories including low review level, reinforcing H1 as the most actionable lever.
- **Spearman robustness check (H1)**: rho = −0.176 (n = 95,824, p ≈ 0) — statistically significant but weak, indicating the review drop is driven by crossing the late/on-time threshold, not by a continuous dose-response effect of delay magnitude.
- Order value is extremely right-skewed (skewness = 9.37); mean (BRL 159.86) sits well above median (BRL 105.28).

## How to Run

1. Go to project folder:

```bash
cd 02_eda_olist_storytelling
```

2. Create and activate a virtual environment (optional if you already use conda):

```bash
python -m venv venv
venv\\Scripts\\activate
```

3. Install dependencies:

```bash
pip install -r requirements.txt
```

4. Configure Kaggle API:
- Create token in Kaggle account settings.
- Save `kaggle.json` in:
  - Windows: `C:\\Users\\<YOUR_USER>\\.kaggle\\kaggle.json`
  - Linux/Mac: `~/.kaggle/kaggle.json`

5. Open and run:

```text
notebooks/02_eda_olist_storytelling.ipynb
```

---

## Notes

- Do not commit credentials (`kaggle.json`) or virtual environment folders.
- Raw Olist CSVs (~121 MB) are downloaded locally and excluded via `.gitignore`; re-run the notebook to regenerate them.
- This project prioritizes business framing (hypotheses-first) and statistical rigor over pure descriptive EDA.
- Correlation is not causation: the late-delivery vs. review-score relationship is strong and consistent, but confounders (customer expectations, product quality) are not ruled out.
