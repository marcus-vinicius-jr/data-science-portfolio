# Evaluation of Generative AI Models in Public Health — Malaria Case Study

> **MBA Capstone — USP/Esalq (Data Science & Analytics, 2025)**  
> Part of [data-science-portfolio](../README.md) — Previous Academic Work section.

This project evaluates the quality of responses generated by 10 LLM-based systems
on the topic of malaria, comparing them against official WHO fact sheets as ground truth.
The study proposes a reusable methodology for auditing generative AI in sensitive
public health contexts.

## Methodology

| Step | Method |
|---|---|
| Readability | Flesch Reading Ease · Flesch–Kincaid Grade Level |
| Text similarity | Cosine Similarity (TF-IDF) · Levenshtein Distance · Jaccard Coefficient |
| Composite ranking | Min–Max normalization across all metrics |
| Clustering | K-Means with Z-score standardization (k selected via Elbow & Silhouette) |
| Correspondence analysis | MCA — 2D static map + interactive 3D map (AI × topic × cluster) |
| Statistical validation | One-way ANOVA (p < 0.001 for all metrics) · Chi-square test |

---

## Key Findings

- Three statistically distinct clusters of models identified (p < 0.001), confirming
  real behavioral patterns rather than random variation.
- Question topic strongly influenced segmentation, indicating that prompt formulation
  drives discursive strategies more than the choice of AI model.
- ScholarGPT and ChatGPT 4.0 achieved the highest composite scores (0.91 and 0.85),
  combining readability, semantic similarity and lexical alignment.
- Proposed methodology is reusable for auditing generative AI in any sensitive
  health or regulatory context.
